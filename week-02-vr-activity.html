🎯 WHAT YOU'RE DOING

You will visit a reconstruction of the Hagia Sophia and Constantinople's walls, place evidence from this week's readings, and argue the week's central question directly: was Byzantium a true continuation of Rome, or something new? This is this week's signature assessment.

💻 WHAT YOU'LL NEED
▶
A VR headset (or desktop/mobile VR client)
▶
Completion of both lessons and both primary sources before starting
▶
About 30 minutes
🔒 HOW TO JOIN
Device: VR headset preferred; desktop/mobile supported.
Session type: Temporary Session (solo).
Session ID: WH9-Q1-W02-VR (posted in Canvas).
Location: Hagia Sophia & Theodosian Walls, Constantinople, 6th century.
Spawn point: You arrive inside the Hagia Sophia beneath the great dome; a Sticky Note gives your first instruction.
📝 WHAT TO DO, IN ORDER
1
Look up at the dome and use the placed image to compare its scale to other ancient buildings you have studied.
2
Walk to the Theodosian Walls exhibit area and examine the interactive map showing Belisarius's reconquest of North Africa and Italy.
3
Open the interactive whiteboard titled “Continuation or Departure?” and place two columns of sticky notes: evidence Byzantium continued Rome (law, titles, reconquest) and evidence it became something new (language, religion-and-throne fused together, geography).
4
Add the line from Justinian's Institutes you read today (“to live honestly, to hurt no one, to give everyone his due”) to whichever column you judge it belongs, and justify the placement with a second sticky note.
5
Record a 90-second to 2-minute spatial recording at the Whiteboard arguing your final verdict: continuation, departure, or a genuine mixture, and why. This is Exhibit 2 of your Frontier Atlas.
📤 WHAT TO SUBMIT

Submit your spatial recording link (or a screen recording) and a one-sentence exhibit caption to the Week 2 VR Activity assignment in Canvas.

Estimated time: About 30 minutes

✅ SUCCESS CRITERIA
☐
Examined both the dome and the reconquest interactive map
☐
Populated both columns of the Whiteboard with specific evidence
☐
Placed and justified the Institutes quotation
☐
Recorded and submitted a clear final verdict with reasoning
👨‍🏫 FACILITATOR NOTE

This is the week's substantive, teacher-graded assessment (Tier 3), so give students real time to work through the Whiteboard before recording. There is no single correct verdict; look for students engaging both columns of evidence rather than picking a side reflexively. Be ready to demonstrate placing a Sticky Note if a student is stuck on the mechanics.
