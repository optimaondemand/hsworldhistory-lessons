💡 BEFORE YOU READ

Fulcher of Chartres accompanied the First Crusade and later settled in the Crusader States, eventually serving as chaplain to King Baldwin I. Writing years later, he reflects on how the crusaders themselves had been transformed by their new lives in the East.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
A History of the Expedition to Jerusalem, Book III, Chapter 37 (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Frances Rita Ryan, 1969 (translation of a public-domain-era Latin text); excerpt.
“For we who were Occidentals have now become Orientals… He who was a Roman or a Frank is now made into a Galilean or a Palestinian… We have already forgotten the places of our birth… Some already possess homes and servants which they have received through inheritance. Some have taken wives not merely of their own people, but Syrians, or Armenians, or even Saracens who have received the grace of baptism… Different languages, now made common, become known to both races, and faith unites those whose forefathers were strangers… He who was born a stranger is now as a native.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Fulcher describes real cultural mixing on the frontier (intermarriage, shared language, new local identity) alongside the wars and massacres you have also read about this week. Bring both sides of this picture into your capstone gallery.
