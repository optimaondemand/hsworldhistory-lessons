Charlemagne, Admonitio Generalis (789)
Primary Source Reading · the course map's “Charter of Modern Thought”
💡 BEFORE YOU READ

This capitulary is the founding charter of the Carolingian Renaissance's educational reform: the document behind the school-founding and text-correction described in today's lesson.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
Admonitio Generalis, selected articles (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. after P. D. King, 1987 (scholarly public-domain-era translation; excerpt paraphrased for classroom use).
“Let schools be established in which boys may learn to read. Correct carefully the Psalms, the notation, the chant, the computus [calendar reckoning], and the grammar in each monastery and bishopric, and the catholic books; because often, though men wish to pray to God properly, they nevertheless pray badly because of the incorrect books… And let there be chosen for this work men who are able to teach, and who will do so gladly… Let care be taken that the schoolmasters themselves live honestly and teach diligently.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Notice the stated reason for correcting texts: not scholarly curiosity, but the concern that “men… pray badly because of the incorrect books.” This is the religious motive driving the intellectual revival: exactly the synthesis today's lesson describes.
