Week 5 · Primary Source 1
Einhard, Life of Charlemagne
Primary Source Reading
💡 BEFORE YOU READ

Einhard was a Frankish scholar raised and educated at Charlemagne's own court, who wrote this biography (modeled deliberately on the Roman biographer Suetonius) shortly after the emperor's death in 814. It is admiring but not without genuine detail.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
Life of Charlemagne, Chapters 22 and 28 (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Samuel Epes Turner, 1880 (public domain). Excerpt.
“He had a broad and strong body, of unusual height, but not exceeding proportion… his gait firm, his whole carriage manly… He used to wear the national, that is, the Frank, dress… He was temperate in eating, and particularly so in drinking, for he abominated drunkenness in anybody, much more in himself…”

“At this time he received the title of Emperor and Augustus… He bore very patiently the jealousy that the assumption of this title excited in the Roman Emperors [of the Byzantines], who took it very ill… He had, however, so great a horror of the title of emperor that, as he himself said, he would never have entered the church on the day the title was conferred, although it was a great feast-day, if he had known beforehand of the design of the Pope.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Einhard is writing to shape Charlemagne's legacy for posterity, not to file a neutral report. His claim about Charlemagne's surprise deserves the same critical eye you would give any source written by an admirer, close to the events, about a very powerful patron.
