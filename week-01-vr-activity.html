🎯 WHAT YOU'RE DOING

You will visit a reconstruction of Rome's Aurelian Walls and the Forum to see the scale of what Alaric's army confronted, then arrange evidence into your first Frontier Atlas exhibit panel: a case for what actually caused the sack of 410.

💻 WHAT YOU'LL NEED
▶
A VR headset (or desktop/mobile VR client)
▶
Completion of Lesson 1 and Lesson 2 before starting
▶
About 25 minutes
🔒 HOW TO JOIN
Device: VR headset preferred; desktop/mobile supported.
Session type: Temporary Session (solo).
Session ID: WH9-Q1-W01-VR (posted in Canvas).
Location: Roman Forum & Aurelian Walls, Late Antiquity.
Spawn point: You will arrive at the Portal Spawn Point just outside the Walls; a Sticky Note there gives your first instruction.
📝 WHAT TO DO, IN ORDER
1
Walk the Aurelian Walls and note their scale using the placed image comparing the walls to a modern building.
2
Open the pre-placed interactive whiteboard titled “Three Causes,” and use the 3D pen or the Drawpad to write the three causes from this week's lessons (Adrianople, decades of failed accommodation, Stilicho's execution) in the order you judge most decisive to least.
3
Place a Sticky Note on the Whiteboard defending your ranking in two or three sentences.
4
Record a 60–90 second spatial recording standing at the Walls, explaining your ranking aloud: this becomes Exhibit 1 of your Frontier Atlas.
📤 WHAT TO SUBMIT

Submit the spatial recording link (or a screen recording of it) to the Week 1 VR Activity assignment in Canvas, along with a one-sentence caption for your exhibit panel.

Estimated time: About 25 minutes

✅ SUCCESS CRITERIA
☐
Walked the Location and used the placed display
☐
Wrote and ranked all three causes on the Whiteboard
☐
Recorded and submitted the spatial recording with a caption
👨‍🏫 FACILITATOR NOTE

Students commonly want to rank causes by which felt most dramatic (the sack itself) rather than most decisive (Stilicho's execution). Encourage them to defend their ranking rather than simply restating the lesson; there is no single correct order, but the reasoning should show real engagement with contingency.
