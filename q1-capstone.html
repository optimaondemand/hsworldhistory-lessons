Quarter 1 Capstone Project
The Frontier Atlas of Christendom, c. 1100–1204
Tier 3 · VR component + written component · due at the end of Quarter 1
🎯 PROJECT OVERVIEW

Across Quarter 1 you built nine VR exhibits, one nearly every week, documenting a people, ruler, or institution on the frontiers of Rome's heirs: the Western Empire's fall, Byzantium's survival, the Anglo-Saxon and Frankish kingdoms, the rise of Islam, the Carolingian world, feudalism and monasticism, England's conquest, the Investiture Controversy, and the Crusades. This capstone asks you to bring all nine together into a single gallery walk and to argue, using the whole quarter's evidence, what actually held medieval Christendom's frontiers together: and what put them most at risk.

🎯 ESSENTIAL QUESTION
Across three centuries of collapse, conquest, and contact, what made a medieval frontier hold, and what made it break?
💻 VR COMPONENT: GALLERY ASSEMBLY
1
Join the persistent Gallery Session at Session ID WH9-Q1-CAPSTONE-VR (posted in Canvas). This is a persistent Session: your arranged gallery will remain in place for your facilitator to review and for classmates in a live section to walk through.
2
Place all nine exhibit markers in the Gallery Location's nine numbered alcoves, using the spatial recordings, snapshots, and Whiteboard notes you produced weekly. Each alcove should display: the exhibit's title, one image or diagram, and your original recorded reflection or a new sticky-note summary of it.
3
Add a tenth, final alcove: “Verdict.” Using an interactive whiteboard, write your answer to the essential question above, citing evidence from at least four different exhibits.
4
Record a 3–4 minute spatial recording walking the full gallery in order, narrating your argument as you pass each alcove and arriving at your verdict at the tenth.
📝 WRITTEN COMPONENT (M365 Word Document)

Write a 500–700 word essay answering the essential question directly, citing specific evidence from at least four exhibits/weeks. Your essay should explicitly engage the Classical Republican standard used throughout this quarter (could each regime or institution keep internal order, administer justice, and defend against external threats?) and should resist any single deterministic explanation: show where contingency mattered.

👨‍🏫 FACILITATOR NOTE

This is the largest single teacher-graded assessment this quarter; budget real SpeedGrader time for it. Students commonly need a reminder that the persistent Session means their gallery stays in place after the session ends: they do not need to rebuild it each time.
