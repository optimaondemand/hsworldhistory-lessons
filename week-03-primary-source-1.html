Gregory of Tours, History of the Franks
Primary Source Reading
💡 BEFORE YOU READ

Gregory, bishop of Tours in Frankish Gaul, wrote his History around 590 to record the deeds (often violent) of the Merovingian Frankish kings descended from Clovis, who had converted to Catholic (not Arian) Christianity around 496, a choice that aligned the Franks religiously with the Gallo-Roman population they ruled.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
History of the Franks, Book II, on the baptism of Clovis (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Ernest Brehaut, 1916 (public domain). Excerpt.
“The queen [Clotilde] did not cease to urge him to confess the true God and forsake idols… And the king said: ‘I have gladly heard thee, but one thing still holds me back, that the people who are under me are not willing to give up their gods.’… Now it came to pass that war arose against the Alamanni… And when the two armies met in fierce battle, there was great slaughter, and Clovis's army began to be in danger of destruction. He saw it, raised his eyes to heaven, and said: ‘Jesus Christ, whom Clotilde declares to be the son of the living God… I invoke thy glory. If thou wilt grant me victory over these enemies… I will believe in thee and be baptized in thy name.’ And when he said thus, the Alamanni turned their backs and began to flee… The king told the queen how he had won the victory by calling on the name of Christ. Then the queen… bade the holy Remigius, bishop of the city, be summoned secretly… And so the king confessed the omnipotent God in the Trinity, and was baptized in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit… More than 3,000 of his army were baptized at the same time.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Compare this account to Kent's conversion in your lesson: in both cases, a king's Christian wife plays the decisive role, and in both cases the king converts along with his whole following at once, not as individuals over time.
