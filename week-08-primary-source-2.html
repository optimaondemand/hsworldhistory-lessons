Week 8 · Primary Source 2
Letters Between Henry IV and Gregory VII
Primary Source Reading
💡 BEFORE YOU READ

These two documents, from opposite sides of the conflict, were both issued in 1076, in the months just before Canossa. Notice the tone each man takes toward the other.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
Henry IV's letter to Gregory VII, 1076 (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Ernest F. Henderson, 1892 (public domain). Excerpt.
“Henry, king not through usurpation but through the holy ordination of God, to Hildebrand, at present not pope but false monk… Thou hast attacked me… thou hast dared to threaten to deprive me… of my kingdom… as if we had received our kingdom from thee, as if the kingdom and the empire were in thine and not in God's hand… Descend, therefore, descend, thou who hast been damned by this our judgment and that of all our bishops; relinquish the apostolic chair which thou hast usurped.”
📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
Gregory VII's sentence of excommunication against Henry IV, 1076 (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Ernest F. Henderson, 1892 (public domain). Excerpt.
“I deprive King Henry… of the government of the whole kingdom of Germany and Italy, and I release all Christians from the bond of the oath which they have made or shall make to him… And because he has scorned to obey as a Christian should, and has not returned to the God whom he had abandoned… and because he has separated himself from the Church… I bind him in the bondage of anathema.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Notice that both men claim to be defending God's own order against the other's illegitimate usurpation. Neither considers himself the aggressor. Keep this in mind when you are asked to evaluate the conflict fairly.
