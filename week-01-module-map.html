🎯 MODULE OBJECTIVES
1
Explain why the Western Roman Empire's collapse was a contingent political and military failure, not an inevitable decline.
2
Trace how the Eastern Empire, refounded by Constantine and reorganized by Justinian, survived as a continuation of Rome under a new name: Byzantium.
3
Evaluate the moral and institutional strengths and weaknesses of both the Western and Eastern courts using the standards of Classical Republican judgment: could each regime keep internal peace, administer justice, and defend its frontier?

🧙 THE FRONTIER ATLAS PROJECT

This is the first module of the Quarter 1 capstone: the Frontier Atlas of Christendom, c. 1100. Each week's VR activity produces one exhibit (an arranged scene, an annotated map, or a recorded reflection) documenting a people or kingdom on the frontier between Rome's heirs and its neighbors. In Week 9 you will assemble your exhibits into a single gallery walk on the eve of the First Crusade. This week's exhibits: Exhibit 1, Rome Besieged (Week 1) and Exhibit 2, Justinian's Constantinople (Week 2).
