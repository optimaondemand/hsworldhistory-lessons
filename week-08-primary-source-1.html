💡 BEFORE YOU READ

This second excerpt from Einhard's biography (you first met it in Week 5) shows Charlemagne exercising direct authority over church affairs and construction: exactly the kind of royal control over the church's leadership and property that would become so contested three centuries later.

📜 PRIMARY SOURCE
Life of Charlemagne, Chapter 26 (excerpted and lightly adapted)
Trans. Samuel Epes Turner, 1880 (public domain). Excerpt.
“He cherished with the greatest fervor and devotion the principles of the Christian religion… He built the beautiful basilica at Aachen, and adorned it with gold and silver and lamps, and with rails and doors of solid brass… He was a constant worshipper at this church as long as his health permitted, going morning and evening… and he took care that all the services there conducted should be administered with the utmost possible propriety, very often warning the sextons not to let any improper or unclean thing be brought into the building or remain in it.”
📌 A NOTE FOR YOU

Charlemagne here acts almost as the church's personal overseer: funding, decorating, staffing, and even policing the conduct of worship at his own chapel. This close royal involvement in church affairs is precisely the pattern later reformers like Gregory VII would come to see as a problem needing correction.
